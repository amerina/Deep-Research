# A-stock Deep Research Methodology

## 8-Step Process
1. **Define Investment Goals**: Outline the objectives and time frames for investment.
2. **Research Companies**: Gather information on potential investment candidates by analyzing their business model, history, and market environment.
3. **Analyze Financial Statements**: Review income statements, balance sheets, and cash flow statements to assess financial health.
4. **Industry Comparison**: Compare the subject company with its industry peers to gauge performance metrics.
5. **Evaluate Market Conditions**: Examine broader economic factors that could affect investment viability.
6. **Risk Assessment**: Identify potential risks involved with investments in chosen companies.
7. **Make Recommendations**: Based on findings, compile investment recommendations with confidence levels.
8. **Monitor Investments**: Regularly review the performance of investments and adjust strategies as necessary.

## Financial Data Verification
- Cross-check financial data from multiple reliable sources such as SEC filings, company reports, and financial news outlets.
- Utilize forensic accounting techniques to uncover discrepancies.

## Regulatory Source Prioritization
- Prioritize sources based on their reliability, relevance, and timeliness.
- Consider SEC filings as primary sources for public company data.

## Quality Control Checklist
- Ensure all data is sourced from reputable and verifiable outlets.
- Maintain consistency in reporting formats and terminologies.
- Review analysis and recommendations for logical consistency and accuracy before finalizing.
